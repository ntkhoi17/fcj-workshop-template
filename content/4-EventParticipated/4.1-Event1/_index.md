# Summary Report: FCAJ Community Day (May 23) 
### Purpose of the Event
The FCAJ Community Day (May 23) event was organized to create a space for sharing knowledge, introducing practical projects, and updating new technology trends within the FCAJ community.
The main objectives of the event included:
* Providing opportunities for members to present technology projects, ideas, and solutions they have researched or deployed.
* Sharing knowledge regarding Generative AI, Large Language Models, Multi-Agent Systems, and their application directions in enterprise environments.
* Introducing solutions for optimizing performance and security of web infrastructures through Amazon CloudFront.
* Spreading real-world experience from Hackathon competitions, particularly how to develop products within a short timeframe.
* Helping attendees expand their perspectives on how to combine technology, product mindset, and teamwork skills.

### List of Speakers & Topics
* **Tinh** - Topic: Build Second Brain
* **Hai Anh** - Topic: Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick
* **Thinh Nguyen** - Topic: From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation
* **Team VIB** - Topic: 36 hrs with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality
* **Dao Duc** - Topic: Deep dive talk: How LLM actually works?
* **Cat Vy** - Topic: Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System: The Case of Startup Credit Scoring

### Key Highlights of the Presentations

#### 1. Build Second Brain - Speaker: Tinh
The presentation focused on methods for building a personal knowledge management system. Instead of fragmented note-taking, learners can organize information in a connected, easy-to-retrieve, and reusable manner.
This content helped me understand that learning technology depends not only on the amount of knowledge received, but also heavily on how that knowledge is stored, systematized, and exploited when needed.

#### 2. Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick - Speaker: Hai Anh
The presentation introduced a direction for building a friendly AI assistant to support users in interacting with and exploiting data more visually. The content emphasized how to apply AWS services to create chatbots capable of analyzing and responding according to user needs.
Through this part, I gained more perspective on how AI applications do not just stop at answering questions, but can also combine with data to support better decision-making.

#### 3. From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation - Speaker: Thinh Nguyen
The presentation explained the role of Amazon CloudFront in distributing content from Edge Locations to Origin Servers. The content focused on how CloudFront helps reduce latency, accelerate resource loading speeds, and contribute to safeguarding backend systems.
This is a highly practical part because CloudFront is a service commonly encountered in modern web architectures, especially when deploying frontends, static websites, or systems with users distributed across multiple regions.

#### 4. 36 hrs with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality - Team VIB
Team VIB shared the process of developing the UTMorpho product within a 36-hour Hackathon. The content covered not only technical aspects but also mentioned how the team brainstormed ideas, assigned roles, handled errors, and finalized the demo in a very short time.
This part clearly showed the pressure of a real competitive environment, while helping me better understand the importance of prioritizing core features, team coordination, and quick decision-making.

#### 5. How LLM actually works? - Speaker: Dao Duc
The presentation went deep into how large language models operate. Concepts such as Transformers, Self-Attention, Pre-training, and Fine-tuning were presented to help attendees better understand the technical foundation behind LLMs.
After this part, I understood that an LLM is not just a text generation tool, but the result of multiple layers of data processing, model training, and fine-tuning to fit specific problems.

#### 6. Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System - Speaker: Cat Vy
The presentation introduced how to build an enterprise-grade Multi-Agent system. Agents can coordinate with each other to handle complex tasks, such as financial analysis or credit scoring for startups.
This content helped me better visualize how AI can be organized into multiple specialized components, instead of just using a single model to process the entire workload.

### Key Takeaways

#### Regarding Generative AI & New Technologies
* Gained more understanding of the technical foundation of LLMs, particularly Transformers and Self-Attention.
* Learned the differences between the initial pre-training phase and the model fine-tuning phase.
* Acquired further knowledge on how to design Multi-Agent systems to serve business problems.
* Recognized that AI can be applied in various fields such as chatbots, financial analysis, process automation, and user support.

#### Regarding Cloud Infrastructure Architecture
* Better understood the role of Amazon CloudFront in optimizing application access speeds.
* Learned how CDNs help reduce load on Origin Servers and improve user experiences.
* Acquired more direction when designing web systems that require faster, more stable, and more secure content delivery.
* Recognized that choosing the appropriate infrastructure architecture directly affects operational performance and costs.

#### Regarding Soft Skills & Working Methodologies
* Learned how to organize personal knowledge using the Second Brain method.
* Understood the importance of systematic note-taking when learning new technologies.
* Gathered experiences on how to work in teams under high-pressure environments.
* Realized that when developing products in a short time, it is necessary to prioritize the MVP and focus on core values.

### Professional Applications
* **Personal knowledge management:** Apply the Second Brain method to store notes, AWS documents, workshops, and lessons learned during the internship process.
* **Web infrastructure optimization:** Research further on how to use Amazon CloudFront for static websites or frontend applications deployed on AWS.
* **Exploring AI Agents:** Continue testing simple chatbot models or agents to support repetitive tasks in studying and working.
* **Rapid product development:** Apply lessons from the Hackathon by focusing on important functionalities first, then expanding and finalizing gradually.
* **Improving teamwork skills:** Pay more attention to task division, time management, and clear communication during project execution.

### Overall Event Experience
The event brought me many practical experiences because the contents did not just stop at theory but also included demos, case studies, and sharing from the speakers' actual deployment processes.
The atmosphere of the sharing session was quite open, and the topics were presented from many different angles: from personal knowledge management, AI assistants, CDNs, Hackathons, to LLMs and Multi-Agent Systems. This helped me get a broader view of how technology is applied in reality.
I was particularly impressed with the sharing sections about LLMs, CloudFront, and Hackathons, as these contents can be directly related to the process of learning AWS, doing workshops, and building personal projects.

### Lessons Learned
1. Learning technology needs to go along with methods for storing and systematizing knowledge.
2. When building a system, it is crucial to clearly understand the real-world problem before choosing the technology.
3. AI and Cloud are increasingly tightly integrated in modern solutions.
4. Effective teamwork requires clear assignment, continuous communication, and quick adaptability.
5. A good product does not necessarily have to be perfect from the start, but needs to have a clear development direction starting from an MVP version.
